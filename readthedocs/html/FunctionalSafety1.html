Functional Safety

Functional Safety Document

 

Introduction

 

The functional Safety Package is a set of tools and processes that helps its users to achieve objectives for their products. A system or piece of equipment that depends on automatic protection has functional safety as part of its overall safety. This automatic protection system must react appropriately to inputs and should react predictably to errors.

 

Why Functional Safety?

 

Functional safety systems are built to automatically stop hazardous failures or to control them when they do. It aids in the creation of systems that can carry out particular tasks effectively even when not designed for them.

Safety compliance

 

Possible Malfunctions

 

There are two possible types of malfunctions that can occur:

  • Those that can be detected by the tool and no output is generated
  • Those that cannot be detected by the tool and:
    • an illegal output is generated, or
    • a legal output is generated but which is functionally incorrect

 

Measures to Detect/Prevent Errors

 

The generated code often goes through linting, simulation, and synthesis before being included in the final product. It does not go directly to the output. And generally, all the compiler, simulation, and synthesis tools used are ISO26262 certified.

 

 

Failure Factors

 

Consider system failures, and failure of an element or item that is caused in a deterministic way during development, production, or maintenance. These failures may be produced by faults that may happen imperceptibly during the lifespan of a system. A variety of circumstances can bring these on, including

  • Random or systematic failures
  • Human error
  • Loss of power supply, etc.

 

Purpose and scope 

 

  1. Document Purpose: 

 

Functional safety for the IDesignSpec tool suite includes a functional safety manual that discusses the usage of the applicable products in the context of a safety-related system. It is intended to be a guide for ASIC and FPGA teams developing IPs, subsystems, or complete SoCs to reach a targeted safety integrity level for their product. The manual contains a list of systematic measures that these teams can adopt for that purpose. 

 

  1. Document Scope: 

 

The document provides a set of guidelines and practices for the safe operation of the products. Users must follow the Safety Manual and adhere to the customer responsibilities defined in the manual, to ensure functional correctness in their developed design as per ISO26262/IEC61508 standards. It also provides necessary assumptions of use cases, diagnostic measures, etc. Overall, the contents of this document are defined by the following: 

 

  • Safety context and safety ideas that were developed throughout the IDesignSpec tool suite development
  • Results of safety analysis, providing details on failures and their analysis
  • Additional safety precautions to guarantee safe use.
